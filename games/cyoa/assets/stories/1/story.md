# The Case of the Cooperative Alibi

## Keywords
- mystery
- alibi
- satire

## Chapter 1
### Title
Soup, Silk, and Sudden Death
### Content
The Belmont ballroom smells of cut lilies, warm butter, and fresh panic. A ribbon of consomme stains the white tablecloth like a bad map. Agatha Pringle arranges mourners into tasteful clusters. Inspector Vale keeps snapping open a broken pocketwatch. His jaw tightens at each click. Near the bar, mirrored cufflinks flash on every waiter.

Everyone at the charity table swears no one moved. Everyone says it too quickly. Felix the head waiter looks rehearsed and pale.
### Choices
1. Pull Felix aside before Agatha can coach him -> 2
2. Ignore the people and study the stain, glass, and reflections -> 3

## Chapter 2
### Title
Felix and the Pocket of Secrets
### Content
Felix laughs before answering, but Vale's watch clicks and the laugh dies. "That was the cue," he whispers. He slips out a narrow ledger full of initials, debts, and hush money. Palmer's line is the thickest. Agatha, he says, drilled staff on one shared story.

He checks the corridor twice. "If they see me with this, I'm finished." Agatha timed rehearsals to the watch click so every account matched the service minute. Felix grips the ledger until the paper buckles. He will either trust you in private or crack in public.
### Choices
1. Promise Felix you will hear him out somewhere private -> 4
2. March Felix toward Vale and let fear loosen his tongue -> 5

## Chapter 3
### Title
The Mathematics of Bad Manners
### Content
You crouch by the table until the room becomes angles. The consomme stain is not at the victim's setting; it begins above and left, where a server would pour. A busboy reaches for the spill; you stop him. A steward snaps that the floor will be cleaned, and you buy ten seconds with Vale's badge. Mirrored cufflinks reflect panorama. This is the poison window: the minute between pour and first sip. During that minute, Isabelle, Palmer's creditor with a foreclosure notice already drafted, was standing.

Vale sees it a beat later and goes still. Agatha sees your face and smiles too carefully. You can test that smile in private, or take this reconstruction back to Felix before anyone rehearses again.
### Choices
1. Test Agatha privately and see who rushes to help her lie -> 4
2. Take your reconstruction to Felix before anyone can rehearse again -> 2

## Chapter 4
### Title
Who Was Worth Saving
### Content
Felix slips into the side room first and lays a slim ledger of initials and debts on the table. Agatha arrives a breath later and Palmer behind her, eyes going to the pages before he can think better of it. Agatha paid to shield Palmer's sister. It was blackmail dressed up as mercy. Palmer turned that protection into murder.

Agatha says Isabelle meant to finish Palmer's family by exposing debts and cutting credit. Felix, in a smaller voice, confirms she paid for silence and called it protection. Palmer dosed Isabelle's cup when Vale's watch clicked the service cue, then sat before anyone looked. "I picked the crime that kept them fed," Palmer says. The case is solved with a knife or trumpet.
### Choices
1. Take the quiet confession route and contain the blackmail damage -> 6
2. Build the loudest possible case and let every secret break in daylight -> 7

## Chapter 5
### Title
A Room That Wants Theater
### Content
You bring Felix straight to Vale and raise your voice before the room can feign deafness. Under pressure Felix blurts that the staff rehearsed one alibi for Agatha, then tries to stop short of the name that matters. The table turns toward you, hungry for spectacle and too eager to clap for certainty.

Agatha glides in with a replacement seating chart and discovers, too late, that the old consomme stain still kills her new fiction. Vale murmurs that one careful minute could produce a confession. Palmer alone does not joke about lateness. He watches the exits.
### Choices
1. Slow down, pin the motive to Palmer, and force a private admission -> 6
2. Deliver your grand theory immediately and enjoy the gasp -> 8

## Chapter 6
### Title
Justice Served, Quietly
### Content
You refuse the ovation and take Palmer into the smaller room with Vale, the ledger, and the clock that no longer needs theatrics. Palmer confesses before sunrise. He poisoned Isabelle to stop the ruin of his sister and her children. Agatha built the false alibi, but not the murder.

Between Felix's ledger and the consomme geometry, the ballroom's first lie finally breaks. Palmer goes to prison. Agatha loses the empire she mistook for mercy. Vale closes his broken watch and says, "For once, you solved something without trying to marry it."

## Chapter 7
### Title
A Perfectly Public Success
### Content
You make the whole ballroom watch while Vale walks Palmer out, mirrored cufflinks winking like verdicts. The confession lands, the ledger opens, and the room learns what Agatha charged for silence.

By noon, three innocent dependents have lost inheritances, schooling, or privacy because the case was solved with a trumpet instead of a knife. On the witness-first route, Felix survives because larger names fall first. Agatha is ruined, Palmer is convicted, and justice arrives in full dress and tramples whoever is underneath it.

## Chapter 8
### Title
The Ovation Before the Collapse
### Content
You give the room an accusation pointed at Agatha. Gasps bloom. Agatha goes white, then furious. Vale moves to contain the scene. You choose spectacle over geometry and ignore the clue: others joked about lateness, but Palmer kept checking the room against Vale's broken watch because its click marked service. He needed that minute to switch Isabelle's cup and only hers.

By dawn, accusation is expensive. Palmer did it to keep his sister's family fed, and your public misfire buys him time. Agatha sues. Palmer vanishes by train. Vale says nothing. You had waiter, stain, and watch. You win the room and lose the case.
### Choices
The End
