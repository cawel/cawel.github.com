# The Case of the Cooperative Alibi

## Keywords
- mystery
- alibi
- satire

## Chapter 1
### Title
Soup, Silk, and Sudden Death
### Content
The Belmont ballroom smells of lilies, butter, and panic. A ribbon of consomme stains the white tablecloth like a map drawn by a drunk cartographer. Agatha Pringle is already arranging mourners into tasteful clusters. Inspector Vale keeps snapping open a broken pocketwatch as if shame might restart it. Near the bar, mirrored cufflinks flash on every waiter. Agatha keeps correcting guests who call themselves fashionably late, which is a strange hobby beside a corpse.

Everyone at the charity table swears no one moved. Everyone says it too quickly. Felix the waiter looks especially rehearsed.
### Choices
1. Pull Felix aside before Agatha can coach him -> 2
2. Ignore the people and study the stain, glass, and reflections -> 3

## Chapter 2
### Title
Felix and the Pocket of Secrets
### Content
Felix laughs before answering, but Vale's pocketwatch clicks and the laugh dies. "That was the cue," he whispers. He slips out a narrow ledger full of initials, debts, and hush money. Palmer's line is the thickest. Agatha, he says, made the staff practice one shared story to protect a ruined family from scandal.

Felix clutches the ledger so hard the paper bends. He will either trust you in private or crack under pressure in public.
### Choices
1. Promise Felix you will hear him out somewhere private -> 4
2. March Felix toward Vale and let fear loosen his tongue -> 5

## Chapter 3
### Title
The Mathematics of Bad Manners
### Content
You crouch by the table until the room becomes angles instead of gossip. The consomme did not fall beside the victim's chair. It fell from above and left of it. In the security glass, the staff's mirrored cufflinks throw back a warped little panorama. Isabelle Crane was standing during the poison window, not seated where everyone swears she sat.

Vale sees it a beat later and goes very still. Agatha sees your face and smiles too carefully. You can test that smile in private, or break the room open and force everyone into the light.
### Choices
1. Test Agatha privately and see who rushes to help her lie -> 4
2. Break the room open and force everyone, including Felix, into the light -> 5

## Chapter 4
### Title
Who Was Worth Saving
### Content
You get Agatha into a side room before the ballroom can recover. Before she can settle into hostess poise, Felix slips through the half-closed door clutching the ledger like contraband scripture. That is the last piece. Agatha had been paying to shield Palmer's sister from ruin. Palmer took the protection further and turned it into murder.

Agatha finally says it plain: Isabelle meant to finish the family, and Palmer stopped her with poison. "I picked the crime that kept them fed," Palmer says, staring at the carpet. The case can now be solved with a knife or with a trumpet.
### Choices
1. Take the quiet confession route and contain the blackmail damage -> 6
2. Build the loudest possible case and let every secret break in daylight -> 7

## Chapter 5
### Title
A Room That Wants Theater
### Content
You bring Felix straight to Vale and raise your voice before the room can pretend not to listen. Under that pressure Felix blurts that the staff rehearsed one alibi for Agatha, then tries to save himself by stopping just short of the name that matters. The whole table turns toward you, eager in the greedy way rich people always are when disaster becomes entertainment.

Agatha glides in with a replacement seating chart and discovers, too late, that the old consomme stain still disproves her new fiction. Vale murmurs that one more careful minute could produce a real confession. Palmer alone does not joke about lateness. He only watches the exits.
### Choices
1. Slow down, pin the motive to Palmer, and force a private admission -> 6
2. Deliver your grand theory immediately and enjoy the gasp -> 8

## Chapter 6
### Title
Justice Served, Quietly
### Content
You refuse the ovation and take Palmer into the smaller room with Vale, the ledger, and the clock that no longer needs theatrics. Palmer confesses before sunrise. He poisoned Isabelle to stop the ruin of his sister and her children. Agatha built the false alibi, but not the murder.

Palmer goes to prison. Quietly. Agatha loses the empire she mistook for mercy. Felix keeps his freedom by finally telling the truth. Vale closes his broken watch and says, "For once, you solved something without trying to marry it."
### Choices
The End

## Chapter 7
### Title
A Perfectly Public Success
### Content
You make the whole ballroom watch while Vale walks Palmer out, mirrored cufflinks winking like tiny verdicts. The confession lands, the ledger opens, and the room finally learns what Agatha charged for silence. You are right in every visible way.

By noon, three innocent dependents have lost inheritances, schooling, or privacy because the case was solved with a trumpet instead of a knife. Agatha is ruined, Palmer is convicted, and Felix survives only because everyone larger has fallen first. Justice arrives in full dress and tramples whoever is underneath it.
### Choices
The End

## Chapter 8
### Title
The Ovation Before the Collapse
### Content
You give the room exactly what it wants: a glittering accusation, pointed at Agatha. Gasps bloom on schedule. Agatha goes white, then furious. Vale moves to contain the scene. In that instant you ignore the clue that mattered most: everyone else joked about lateness, but Palmer kept checking the room against Vale's broken watch because he needed the exact minute, not the style of it.

By the time the mistake is clear, dawn has made accusation expensive and pursuit slow. Agatha sues. Palmer vanishes with the morning trains. Vale does not say "I told you so," which is the cruelest thing he could possibly do. You won the room for thirty splendid seconds and lost the case for good.
### Choices
The End
