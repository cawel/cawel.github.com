# The Last Broadcast

## Keywords
- space
- signal
- survival

## Chapter 1
### Title
Silence Over Earth

### Content
You are the communications specialist aboard an orbital station when every Earth channel drops at 03:12 UTC. No static. No distress traffic. No ground control heartbeat. Through the cupola, city grids darken in sweeping waves. Then one nonhuman signal appears, repeating coordinates in northern Canada.

### Choices
1. Reply to the signal and attempt structured contact -> 2
2. Ignore it and begin emergency re-entry planning -> 3

## Chapter 2
### Title
Prime Number Voice

### Content
You answer with prime numbers. The signal mirrors your sequence, then prints text on your console: "DO NOT LAND YET." It claims to be a contingency intelligence monitoring a rapid atmospheric adaptation event on Earth. It says survivors are converging at the transmitted coordinates and that your orbital sensor data could determine policy at a crisis council.

### Choices
1. Collect independent orbital evidence before committing -> 4
2. Treat the signal as hostile and descend immediately -> 3

## Chapter 3
### Title
Forced Descent

### Content
You deorbit through unstable weather corridors and hear fragmented human traffic during plasma blackout. On reentry exit, the sky is amber and particulate-heavy. You can divert to cold-water splashdown for survival odds or continue to a damaged land corridor where survivor convoys were last seen.

### Choices
1. Divert to emergency ocean splashdown -> 8
2. Continue to the land corridor and seek survivors -> 5

## Chapter 4
### Title
Evidence Window

### Content
You spend six tense hours on multispectral scans. Results confirm a novel isotope rise in the atmosphere, migration clusters converging on the coordinates, and active multilingual broadcasts referencing a "Convergence Council." The crisis is real, global, and organized. Your data could validate or challenge radical decisions on human adaptation.

### Choices
1. Descend to the council and testify with your data -> 6
2. Stay in orbit longer and archive events remotely -> 9

## Chapter 5
### Title
Ground Convoy

### Content
You land hard but alive on a crystalline plain where improvised survivor convoys are moving toward the same northern coordinates. Their leader, Ilyas, tells you two blocs are deadlocked: one wants forced biological transformation for immediate survival, the other demands consent-based adaptation with protected baseline enclaves. They need your orbital evidence to break the stalemate.

### Choices
1. Travel with Ilyas to the council chamber -> 6
2. Attempt direct sabotage of adaptation towers yourself -> 8

## Chapter 6
### Title
The Convergence Vote

### Content
At the council, hybrid envoys and human delegates argue over one question: does survival justify removing consent? You are the only witness with full-spectrum orbital data covering onset rates and regional variance. Your testimony will either enable a negotiated timeline or justify forced acceleration.

### Choices
1. Publish all data publicly and force a consent-based treaty -> 10
2. Endorse rapid mandatory transformation for maximal speed -> 7

## Chapter 7
### Title
Survival Without Choice

### Content
You support mandatory transformation. Mortality drops quickly, but resistance zones are erased and dissent is neurologically dampened "for stability." Humanity persists as a species while losing essential pluralism in how it thinks, grieves, and chooses. History calls you the witness who saved billions. Survivors call you the voice that signed away consent.

### Choices
The End

## Chapter 8
### Title
The Last Burn

### Content
You run sabotage with incomplete tools against infrastructure built to resist military strikes. The towers vent ionized particulates and your filters fail. You use your final minutes to broadcast maps and hazard signatures for others. Your pulse line ends in static before rescue can arrive.

### Choices
The End

## Chapter 9
### Title
Orbit of One

### Content
You remain above and keep recording as supplies thin and transmissions fade. Your archive becomes the most complete witness record of Earth’s transition, but you never return to the world you documented. Months later, your station drifts silent with your data cache still cycling toward any receiver that might listen.

### Choices
The End

## Chapter 10
### Title
The Human Clause

### Content
You release your complete dataset to every negotiating bloc and public channel at once. The evidence proves there is a narrow but real window for phased adaptation. Under global scrutiny, the council ratifies the Human Clause: no involuntary modification, sanctuary zones for baseline populations, and independent scientific oversight. The transition stays hard, but choice survives—and so does your faith that survival and dignity can coexist.

### Choices
The End
