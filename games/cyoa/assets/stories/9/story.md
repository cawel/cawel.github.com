# The Fracture Hour

## Keywords
- belt
- loop
- contact

## Chapter 1
### Title
The Alarm You've Heard Before

### Content
You jolt awake with the same copper taste in your mouth and the same station alarm cycling red. Varda Station, asteroid belt. 04:07 ship-time. Your bunk is on the third ring, your jump-suit is draped over the same chair, your half-eaten ration bar is still on the shelf.

You have been here before. Many times.

In two hours and forty minutes, a Fracture cluster will punch through the observation deck, kill seventeen people, and scatter station debris across cargo ring C. The first time you died in the initial breach, screaming into a suit that wouldn't seal. After that, you started waking up here—always this same second, always with the memories of every previous loop intact.

Whatever is causing the reset, it lives on Varda. And you are running out of patience.

### Choices
1. Go straight to Commander Voss in ops and force him to listen -> 2
2. Find Dara in engineering—she has been close to cracking the artifact readings -> 3

## Chapter 2
### Title
The Commander's Price

### Content
Voss is running pre-shift ops with three subordinates when you push through the hatch. You tell him a Fracture cluster will breach the observation deck at 06:47. He sets down his coffee, studies your face, and tells you to sleep it off.

You have burned loops on Voss before. You know what will move him and what won't. Rank won't. Pleading won't. But precision will.

You have two options left.

### Choices
1. Give Voss the exact breach coordinates and timing down to the second -> 4
2. Leave Voss behind and arm the construction workers in ring C yourself -> 5

## Chapter 3
### Title
Dara's Eureka

### Content
Dara is bent over artifact sensor feeds when you find her, surrounded by empty ration packs and cold coffee. This loop she looks up at you differently—as if she almost expected someone to burst in with wild eyes.

She turns the screen toward you. A waveform pulses in a clean ascending pattern, tighter and tighter. The artifact isn't just alien, she says. It's counting down to something. Each loop the oscillation peaks a fraction higher. Whatever it is converging toward, it will arrive soon.

She is the first person in any loop who has reached this conclusion. You feel the hairs on your forearms rise.

### Choices
1. Help Dara isolate the artifact before the Fracture can sync with it -> 6
2. Ask Dara whether the countdown could be redirected—used as a weapon against the Fracture -> 5

## Chapter 4
### Title
Credibility Check

### Content
You tell Voss the strike comes at 06:47:22, through the observation deck's outer pane, from bearing 224 declination negative twelve. You describe the sound the hull makes four seconds before impact—a low harmonic resonance that the pressure sensors will log but no one will recognize until it's too late.

Then you wait.

At 06:47:22, the proximity alarm screams. Voss stares at you across the ops table, jaw tight, every second of skepticism suddenly absent. Outside, the station's perimeter array lights up with inbound contacts at exactly the bearing you named.

"Talk fast," he says.

### Choices
1. Lead a coordinated intercept team to the observation deck -> 7
2. Use Voss's authority to seal the artifact vault and force an overload -> 8

## Chapter 5
### Title
The Cascade

### Content
Good intentions collide in the cargo corridors. Armed construction workers, an engineering team rerouting power to the artifact array, and station security responding to an unauthorized weapons alert—three factions moving fast and blind, each certain they are the only ones acting rationally.

The artifact pulses three times in rapid succession. A frequency you have never recorded in any previous loop. The walls vibrate and your vision smears at the edges. Somewhere in the engineering bay, a pressure coupling blows.

The Fracture hasn't even arrived yet. You are already losing the station from the inside.

### Choices
1. Sprint to the artifact room and contain the pulse before it cascades further -> 6
2. Run for the emergency pods before the structural failure cascade makes escape impossible -> 9

## Chapter 6
### Title
The Artifact Room

### Content
The artifact floats in its magnetic cradle: a latticed sphere of a material that no spectral analysis has ever categorized. Up close it hums against your sternum like a second heartbeat.

Dara's readings confirm what you have suspected but never had proof of until now. The artifact is the anchor point of the loop. Every time the Fracture reaches it, it rewinds time—not as an attack, but as a fail-safe. The loop's logic is preservation: if the artifact changes hands, reality resets until it doesn't.

The Fracture isn't invading Varda Station. It is trying to retrieve something it lost a very long time ago.

Now you have to decide how you want this to end.

### Choices
1. Sever the artifact's power coupling to collapse the loop without destroying the artifact -> 10
2. Overload the artifact in a controlled detonation—ending the loop and the Fracture threat permanently -> 8

## Chapter 7
### Title
Contact

### Content
Your intercept team meets the Fracture at the observation deck seconds before the outer pane cracks. Suits sealed, magnetic anchors set, weapons charged—and then you do something no previous loop allowed time for: you play back the artifact's recorded resonance signature over the hull's external emitter array.

The Fracture entities halt their advance.

Eleven slow minutes pass. No words, no shared language, no protocol for this. But on the hull sensors, their formation changes from assault spread to something else—something closer to waiting.

The artifact pulses once, softly, and goes dark.

The loop does not reset.

You stand in the observation deck watching the Fracture cluster hold position in the dark outside the glass, and you understand: they were not attacking. They were answering a distress beacon embedded in the artifact for longer than humanity has traveled this far from Earth. You are the first person who has ever understood that.

You key your comm. "Hold your fire. All units hold your fire."

The silence that follows is the first new moment you have experienced in a very long time.

### Choices
The End

## Chapter 8
### Title
Controlled Burn

### Content
The artifact detonates in a contained implosion, venting exotic radiation down the magnetically isolated vault. The Fracture cluster collapses. The loop, stripped of its anchor, ends.

Varda Station survives intact. Seventeen people who died in every previous loop walk out of the observation deck unaware of how many times they almost didn't.

But the detonation costs Dara her life. She stays at the containment console to hold the magnetic fields in alignment and refuses your order to run. The explosion is clean and fast. You are told that is something.

Six weeks later, the station files its incident report. The artifact, the loop, the Fracture—all classified under a corporate extraction charter. You give testimony that no one will declassify for thirty years. The people whose lives you saved will never know. Dara's name does not appear in any public record.

The loop is over. The price was real.

### Choices
The End

## Chapter 9
### Title
The Last Loop

### Content
You sprint for the emergency pods but the cascade severs the umbilical locks before you reach ring two. The station groans in a register you have never heard before—not the sharp crack of a Fracture breach but something deeper, structural, permanent.

In your final four seconds you realize this is different. There is no copper taste building at the back of your throat. No sense of the reset pulling at the edges of your consciousness.

The artifact has burned out. Whatever was sending you back has spent its last pulse. The loop's anchor is gone.

This is not a reset. There is no next loop.

Varda Station is gone.

### Choices
The End

## Chapter 10
### Title
Severance

### Content
Dara cuts the power coupling with precise, practiced hands while you hold the magnetic cradle steady against the artifact's resistance. For one long second nothing happens. The hum against your sternum dies, then the resonance signature on her screen drops flat.

Outside, the Fracture cluster completes its approach—and then disperses. Without the beacon, there is nothing to home on. They drift apart into the dark like they have somewhere else to be.

Varda Station runs full damage assessments on a breach that never came.

Dara looks at you, confused. "You okay? You look like you've been awake for a week."

"Longer than that," you say.

You sit down on the floor of the artifact room and write down everything you remember—every loop, every death, every small thing you learned to do differently. You sign your name at the bottom. The debrief takes five weeks and the station council votes to publish the full artifact record.

Whatever the Fracture was looking for, they deserve to know their message was received. Neither side can yet translate it. But the message exists, and so do you, and for now that is enough.

### Choices
The End
