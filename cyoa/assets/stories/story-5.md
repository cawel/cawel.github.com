# The Sunken Library

## Keywords
- library
- tides
- secrets

## Chapter 1
### Title
Steps Below the Harbor

### Content
You descend the barnacled steps that appear only at extreme low tide, carrying a storm ledger for your harbor council. The council sent you because hurricanes have grown deadlier each season, and rumor says the Sunken Library preserves a weather codex that can predict impossible tides. At the bronze doors, a carved warning reads: "When the third bell tolls, leave or be cataloged."

### Choices
1. Search the mapped halls for the weather codex -> 2
2. Explore the restricted abyss wing for faster answers -> 3

## Chapter 2
### Title
Cartographer’s Hall

### Content
You enter a vaulted chamber lined with tide maps stitched on whale-skin. A translucent librarian appears and asks your intent. You answer truthfully: your harbor is running out of time, and you need forecasts to keep people alive. The spirit points toward the lighthouse vault where the codex is kept.

### Choices
1. Follow the spirit’s direction to the lighthouse vault -> 4
2. Ignore it and seek forbidden shortcuts in the abyss wing -> 3

## Chapter 3
### Title
Abyss Wing

### Content
The abyss wing is colder and deeper, with chained volumes that hum when you pass. One iron-bound book promises complete mastery of hidden currents if you read it now without pause. You remember the warning at the entrance and feel the second bell shudder through the walls.

### Choices
1. Read the forbidden volume for immediate total knowledge -> 6
2. Refuse and return to pursue the codex properly -> 2

## Chapter 4
### Title
Lighthouse Vault

### Content
You surface into a dry vault beneath the old lighthouse lens. The shell-bound weather codex rests on a pedestal beside a note: "Knowledge that saves must be shared, or it turns to tyranny." You can leave now with the codex and publish methods for all captains, or hide it for selective control.

### Choices
1. Study quickly, then return to share forecasts openly -> 5
2. Take the codex in secret and keep control private -> 7

## Chapter 5
### Title
The Reading Chamber

### Content
In a pearl-lit chamber, you extract practical protocols: pressure-drop triggers, route windows, and harbor warning thresholds fishers can use without arcane tools. The third bell is close. You can still exit with a complete public plan if you move now.

### Choices
1. Escape with the codex plan and brief the council -> 8
2. Stay to gather one more hidden chart for personal leverage -> 7

## Chapter 6
### Title
Cataloged

### Content
You open the forbidden book and your mind floods with too many currents at once. Direction, depth, temperature, pressure—everything all at once, nothing sortable. When the third bell tolls, water surges through the wing and your lantern dies. Your voice remains in the stacks as a cautionary whisper for the next desperate reader.

### Choices
The End

## Chapter 7
### Title
Keeper in the Dark

### Content
You keep the codex private and use it to enrich your own fleet while rival captains sail blind. For a while, your power grows. Then trust in the harbor collapses. When the black-season superstorm finally arrives, no one believes your late warning call. You survive behind reinforced glass. Most of the old docks do not.

### Choices
The End

## Chapter 8
### Title
Harbor of Lanterns

### Content
You publish the codex methods, train crews in open workshops, and build a shared stormwatch network across every pier. The next hurricane season is brutal, but coordinated departures and shelter timing save hundreds. Children later grow up treating emergency bulletins as common civic craft, not secret elite knowledge. You entered the library to find power. You left with responsibility—and your harbor lived.

### Choices
The End
